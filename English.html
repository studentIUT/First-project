Basic Arithmetic.

Listen regularly:
Make listening to English a daily habit. This could include listening to podcasts, audiobooks, radio shows, or music.
Choose a variety of accents and speakers:
English is spoken in various accents around the world. Exposure to different accents will help you understand a range of English speakers. You can find podcasts or videos with speakers from different English-speaking countries.
Watch movies and TV shows:
Watching movies and TV shows in English can be an enjoyable way to improve your listening skills. Try using subtitles to help you understand the dialogue.

Vocabulary is the cornerstone of effective communication, serving as the building blocks that construct our thoughts and expressions. It encompasses a diverse array of words, ranging from the everyday to the specialized, each carrying its own unique nuance and meaning. A rich vocabulary not only facilitates clearer and more precise communication but also enhances the depth and subtlety of expression.

Long division.

In arithmetic, long division is a standard division algorithm suitable for dividing multi-digit Hindu-Arabic numerals (Positional notation) that is simple enough to perform by hand. It breaks down a division problem into a series of easier steps. As in all division problems, one number, called the dividend, is divided by another, called the divisor, producing a result called the quotient. It enables computations involving arbitrarily large numbers to be performed by following a series of simple steps.[1] The abbreviated form of long division is called short division, which is almost always used instead of long division when the divisor has only one digit.
